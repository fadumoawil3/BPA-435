Open Minds, Open Hearts, Open Doors

Mood Disorders

Some types of mood disorders are Depression (major depression disorder), Bipolar Disorder, & Cyclothymic Disorder. Depression involves persistent sadness and loss of interest. Bipolar Disorder brings extreme mood, energy, and activity shifts. Cyclothymic Disorder causes alternating periods of mild depression and hypomanic symptoms for at least two years.

Anxiety Disorders

Some types of anxiety disorders are Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety Disorder, Phobias (agoraphobia), Phobias (agoraphobia, specific phobias), & Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD). These disorders involve excessive fear, worry, or avoidance. Symptoms may include persistent anxiety, sudden panic attacks, intense fear in social situations, specific fears of objects or situations, and repetitive thoughts or behaviors that disrupt daily life.

Psychotic Disorders

Some psychotic disorders are Schizophrenia, Schizoaffective Disorder, & Brief Psychotic Disorder. These disorders are marked by distorted thinking, perceptions, and emotions. Symptoms can include hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech or behavior, and difficulties distinguishing reality from imagination.

Personality Disorders

Some examples of personality disorders are Borderline Personality Disorder, Antisocial Personality Disorder, Narcissistic Personality Disorder, & Avoidant Personality Disorder. These disorders involve enduring patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that deviate from cultural expectations. Symptoms can include unstable relationships, impulsivity, lack of empathy, excessive self-importance, or extreme sensitivity to criticism and social avoidance.

Eating Disorders

Some examples of eating disorders are Anorexia Nervosa, Bulimia Nervosa, & Binge-Eating Disorder. These disorders involve unhealthy relationships with food, body image, and eating behaviors. Symptoms may include extreme food restriction, cycles of bingeing and purging, or frequent episodes of overeating, often leading to emotional distress and physical health issues.

Trauma and Stress-Related

Some examples of these two types of disorders are Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD), Acute Stress Disorder, & Adjustment Disorders. These disorders are triggered by stressful or traumatic events. Symptoms can include intrusive memories, avoidance, mood changes, heightened arousal, and difficulty coping with everyday life following the event.

Neurodevelopmental

Some types of Neurodevelopmental Disorders are Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), & Learning Disorder. These disorders begin in childhood and affect development, behavior, or learning. Symptoms may include difficulties with attention, social interaction, or academic skills, often impacting daily functioning and more.

Substance and Addictive

Some types of substance-related and addictive disorders are Alcohol Use Disorder, Drug Use Disorder, & Gambling Disorder. These disorders involve problematic patterns of substance use or addictive behaviors. Symptoms can include cravings, loss of control, continued use despite negative consequences, and difficulties fulfilling responsibilities at work, school, or home.

Somatic Sx and Related

Some types of somatic sypmptons and related disorders are Somatic Symptom Disorder and Illness Anxiety Disorder (Hypochondria). These disorders involve excessive focus on physical symptoms or health concerns. Symptoms may include persistent bodily complaints, high health anxiety, and frequent medical visits, often without a clear medical cause.

Dissociative Disorder

Some types of dissociative disorders are Dissociative Identity Disorder and Depersonalization/Derealization Disorder. These disorders involve disruptions in memory, identity, or perception. Symptoms may include feeling detached from oneself or reality, memory gaps, or having distinct identities or personality states.

Other Areas

Others types or disorder as relating to mental health there are Sleep-Wake Disorders (insomnia), the Sexual and Gender Identity Disorder, & Impulse Control Disorders (kleptomania). These disorders affect sleep patterns, sexual functioning, gender identity, or impulse control. Symptoms may include difficulty sleeping, distress about sexual or gender identity, or repeated urges to perform impulsive acts like stealing.
